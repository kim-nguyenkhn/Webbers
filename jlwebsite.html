COMMERCIAL REAL ESTATE WEBSITE
2017



POSITION

WEB DESIGNER




TOOLS & SKILLS USED

SKETCH - ZEPLIN - DGLUX5




OBJECTIVE

Controlco was given the contract of creating an interface for another commercial real estate company. This client is one of the largest real estate corporations in the world, owning over 200 properties internationally. To start, the client gave us five buildings to integrate using our systems. Their website would serve as a dashboard to show live data and graphs without having any writable points. This was the first project that we took on without having to worry about programming setpoints and other values.



The information in this dashboard is dynamic and live; users can quickly view data of each property or region.



APPROACH

I took this design in a whole new direction by introducing a side navigation bar as opposed to a horizontal menu. It was the perfect opportunity to incorporate a new look and feel because the interface was much simpler compared to what we're used to building. The side navigation bar made the website easier to use as a dashboard.



A wireframe sketch. The dashboard home is the first page that the user lands on after logging in. It provides key information about each section of the property.


Can't go wrong with round thin fonts!


The website would be viewed from two levels: regular user and admin. Regular users can only view the properties that they have access to, while admin users can view properties in region-wide levels. The client only wanted me to work on the property-specific version of the dashboard. This assignment was a pitch to the client to gain their trust with more of their properties, so I had to make sure the mock-up was enticing.



After several meetings with our sales rep and clients, we were able to confirm what data needed to be shown. After sending in the final prototype, my clients showed it to the president of the company who in turn approved of it a few hours later. It was a remarkable achievement because my design was going to be the standard for their three thousand properties.



The completed property level dashboard.
