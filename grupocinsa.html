GRUPO CINSA LIGHTING DASHBOARD
2017



POSITION

WEB DESIGNER & DEVELOPER




TOOLS & SKILLS USED

SKETCH - DGLUX5




OBJECTIVE

Grupo Cinsa, client we've been working with for some time, recently began development on several new corporate buildings in Mexico. What they requested from us was a dashboard on the website that would allow their technicians to control their lighting panels, see statuses of circuits, and change the descriptions of certain panels.




Clients will be able to view the lighting panels and corresponding circuits for each of their corporate buildings.



APPROACH

My primary goal was to come up with a concept that will limit human error because this dashboard would contain live data and functional buttons. To prevent users from changing settings by accident, I planned to make one part of the page editable. My concept included having the lighting panels on the left (which are not directly editable) and their corresponding circuits on the right.







Mockup produced in Sketch.



THE CHALLENGE

The most difficult part of this assignment was making the dashboard dynamic— to make the right circuits pop up, show the updated descriptions, allow the buttons to change in the backend, etc. At Controlco, we use an application development tool called DGLux5 to build these systems for our clients. While it doesn't require the knowledge of coding and language, it does require critical thinking skills to put together logic and conditions to make everything work. After completion, the dashboard was our standard to use among all the properties of this client.




The completed dashboard as viewed from a desktop and tablet device.
